# MMOL Canonical Lexicon

## Sensory Channels
The sensory channels refer to the modalities through which information is perceived and processed in the MMOL execution engine. The following sensory channels are defined:

1. **Visual**: Pertaining to sight; includes processing of shapes, colors, and motion.
2. **Auditory**: Related to hearing; encompasses sounds, tones, and rhythms.
3. **Tactile**: Involves the sense of touch; relates to pressure, texture, and temperature.
4. **Olfactory**: Associated with the sense of smell; refers to the detection of odors.
5. **Gustatory**: Involves the sense of taste; includes flavors and taste sensations.

## Representation Modes
Representation modes define how information is represented within the MMOL framework. The key modes are:

1. **Symbolic**: Abstract representation using symbols and signs (e.g., language, mathematical symbols).
2. **Imagistic**: Visualization through images, diagrams, or videos capturing the essence of information.
3. **Motoric**: Physical representation through actions or movements, indicating processes or dynamics.

## Four-Operator Core
The four-operator core describes the fundamental operations that can be performed in the execution engine:

1. **Select**: The ability to choose items or elements from a set based on certain criteria or conditions.
2. **Transform**: Modifying or changing an item in its state or properties (e.g., altering a visual or sound).
3. **Evaluate**: Assessing or judging items against established standards or metrics.
4. **Execute**: Carrying out a command or action based on selected and transformed elements.

## Behavioral Operators
Behavioral operators define the actions that the execution engine can perform including:

1. **Respond**: Reacting to stimuli or inputs based on predefined rules or learned behaviors.
2. **Initiate**: Starting a process or action, driving the system into a new state.
3. **Adapt**: Adjusting behavior based on feedback or changes in the environment.
4. **Terminate**: Concluding an action or stopping a process.

These concepts provide a structured framework for the MMOL execution engine, enabling it to process, interpret, and react to a variety of inputs effectively.
